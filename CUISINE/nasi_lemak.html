Coconut Milk Rice (Nasi Lemak)

Malaysia | By Aisha Tan

Coconut Milk Rice, or known as nasi lemak, is a dish commonly found in Malaysia, where it is considered the national dish. This dish is originating in Malay cuisine that consists of fragrant rice cooked in coconut milk and pandan leaf. Nasi lemak is considered as a typical Malay-style breakfast, making it an essential dish in Malaysia.

Recipe Ingredients

Rice

  • 2 cups coconut milk
  • 2 cups water
  • 2 cups long grain rice, rinsed and drained
  • 1 (1/2 inch) piece fresh ginger, peeled and thinly sliced
  • ¼ teaspoon ground ginger
  • 1 whole bay leaf
  • Salt to taste

Garnish

  • 1 cup oil for frying
  • 1 cup raw peanuts
  • 1 package white anchovies, washes
  • 4 large hard-boiled eggs, peeled and halved
  • 1 medium cucumber, sliced

Sauce

  • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil
  • 1 medium onion, sliced
  • 3 medium shallots, thinly sliced
  • 3 cloves garlic, thinly sliced
  • 2 teaspoons chile paste
  • 1 tablespoon water, or more as needed (Optional)
  • 1 package white anchoives, washed
  • ¼ cup tamarind juice
  • 3 tablespoons white sugar
  • Salt to taste

Recipe Instruction

  1. Make the rice: Stir coconut milk, water, rice, fresh ginger, ground ginger, bay leaf, and salt together in a medium saucepan. Cover and bring to a boil over medium heat. Reduce the heat and simmer until tender, 20 to 30 minutes. Discard bay leaf and keep rice warm until garnish and sauce are ready.
  2. Make the garnish: Heat 1 cup vegetable oil in a large skillet over medium-high heat. Stir in peanuts and cook briefly, until lightly browned. Remove peanuts with a slotted spoon and place onto paper towels to soak up excess grease.
  3. Return the skillet to the stove. Stir in anchovies and cook, turning occasionally, until crisp, 2 to 3 minutes. Remove with a slotted spoon and place on paper towels. Discard oil and wipe out the skillet.
  4. Make the sauce: Heat oil in the clean skillet. Stir in onion, shallots, and garlic; cook until fragrant, 1 to 2 minutes. Mix in chile paste and cook for 10 minutes, stirring occasionally; if mixture is too dry, add water as needed. Stir in anchovies and cook for 5 minutes. Stir in tamarind juice, sugar, and salt; simmer until sauce is thick, about 5 minutes.
  5. Ladle warm rice into bowls. Top with warm sauce, then top with peanuts, fried anchovies, hard-boiled eggs, and cucumber.

Comment From Aisha Tan

Nasi Lemak is truly the heart and soul of Malaysian cuisine. Growing up in Penang, I was fortunate to experience the incredible variety of this dish, from street vendors to family gatherings.
For me, the essence of Nasi Lemak lies in the harmony of its components—the fragrant coconut rice, the spicy sambal, crispy fried anchovies, roasted peanuts, and fresh cucumber.
Each element plays a crucial role, creating a delightful balance of flavors and textures. It’s a dish that never ceases to inspire creativity and showcases the richness of Malaysian culinary traditions.
